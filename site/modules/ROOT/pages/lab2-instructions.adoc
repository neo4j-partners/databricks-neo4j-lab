= Lab 2 Instructions: Databricks ETL to Neo4j

Follow these steps to load aircraft data from Databricks into Neo4j using the Spark Connector.

TIP: For concepts and background, see xref:lab2.adoc[Lab 2: Concepts and Reference].

**Duration:** ~45 minutes

== Prerequisites

* Neo4j Aura credentials from Lab 1 (URI, username, password)
* Databricks workspace invitation email from your workshop admin

== Notebooks

This lab has two notebooks. Run both before proceeding.

[cols="2,4,1"]
|===
| Notebook | Description | Required For

| `01_aircraft_etl_to_neo4j.ipynb`
| Core ETL -- loads Aircraft, System, and Component nodes using the Spark Connector
| Labs 3, 4

| `02_load_neo4j_full.ipynb`
| Full dataset -- adds Sensors, Airports, Flights, Delays, Maintenance Events, and Removals

== Step 1: Accept the Workspace Invitation

1. Click the link in the invitation email to open the Databricks sign-in page.
2. Select **Sign in with email**.
3. Databricks sends a one-time passcode (OTP) to your email address.
4. Check your inbox, copy the 6-digit code, and enter it on the sign-in page.

TIP: The passcode expires after a few minutes. If it expires, click **Resend code** to get a new one.

NOTE: Sometimes after entering the passcode you may see an error. Simply refresh the page and you will be logged in.

Your workshop admin has pre-configured a personal cluster with Apache Spark, the Neo4j Spark Connector, and Python packages needed by the lab notebooks.

1. Click **Compute** in the left sidebar.

== Step 4: Clone the Lab Notebooks

1. Click **Workspace** in the left sidebar.
2. Expand **Shared > databricks-neo4j-lab**.
3. Right-click on the **Lab_2_Databricks_ETL_Neo4j** folder and select **Clone**.

Then:

1. Update the **New name** to include your initials (e.g., `labs-rk`).
2. Select the **For you** tab, choose your home directory, and click **Clone**.

== Step 5: Attach Your Compute Cluster

1. Open `01_aircraft_etl_to_neo4j.ipynb` from your cloned folder.
2. Click the **compute selector** in the top-right corner.
3. Under **Active resources**, select your personal cluster.

NOTE: Do not use **Serverless** compute -- it does not have the Neo4j Spark Connector installed.

image::lab2-change-compute.png[Select your personal compute cluster]

== Step 6: Run Notebook 01

1. Scroll to the **Configuration** cell and enter your Neo4j Aura credentials:
+
[source,python]
----
NEO4J_URI = "neo4j+s://xxxxxxxx.databases.neo4j.io"
NEO4J_USERNAME = "neo4j"
NEO4J_PASSWORD = "<your-password>"
----
+
IMPORTANT: The URI must start with `neo4j+s://` (the `+s` enables TLS encryption required by Aura).

2. Click **Run All** in the notebook toolbar.

== Step 7: Run Notebook 02

Open `02_load_neo4j_full.ipynb` from your cloned folder:

1. Attach your compute cluster
2. Enter your Neo4j credentials
3. Set `CLEAR_DATABASE = True` for a clean load (recommended)
4. Click **Run All**

== Step 8: Explore in Neo4j Aura

1. Open https://console.neo4j.io[console.neo4j.io] in a new browser tab
2. Sign in and select your instance
3. Click **Query** to open the query interface
4. Try a sample query:
+
[source,cypher]
----
MATCH (a:Aircraft)-[:HAS_SYSTEM]->(s:System)-[:HAS_COMPONENT]->(c:Component)
WHERE a.tail_number = 'N95040A'
RETURN a.tail_number, s.name, collect(c.name) AS components
----

== What You Loaded

=== Notebook 01: Core Graph Structure

----
(Aircraft) -[:HAS_SYSTEM]-> (System) -[:HAS_COMPONENT]-> (Component)
----

=== Notebook 02: Full Dataset

----
(Aircraft) -[:HAS_SYSTEM]-> (System) -[:HAS_SENSOR]-> (Sensor)
(Aircraft) -[:OPERATES_FLIGHT]-> (Flight) -[:DEPARTS_FROM / :ARRIVES_AT]-> (Airport)
(Flight) -[:HAS_DELAY]-> (Delay)
(Component) -[:HAS_EVENT]-> (MaintenanceEvent)
(Aircraft) -[:HAS_REMOVAL]-> (Removal) -[:REMOVED_COMPONENT]-> (Component)
----

== Troubleshooting

**"Connection refused" or timeout errors** -- Verify your Neo4j URI starts with `neo4j+s://`, check your instance is running, confirm credentials are correct.

**"Spark Connector not found"** -- Ensure you are using the workshop cluster (not Serverless). The cluster must be in Dedicated (Single User) access mode.

**"Path does not exist" for data files** -- Verify the DATA_PATH matches your workshop configuration. Ask your instructor for the correct Volume path.

**Duplicate nodes appearing** -- The notebook uses Overwrite mode. If needed, clear your database: `MATCH (n) DETACH DELETE n`
